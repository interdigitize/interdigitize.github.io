What is Interdigitize?

Contemporary artists working cross-disciplinary and collaboratively on concepts and projects that they care about.

Started by Kärstin, a classically trained pianist, and Kamie, a storytelling creative.

PROJECTS

A Boy and His Dream

New from the studio this week, the beginning of a new song and an ASCII art music video.

Cycle Cycle Cyc

Some friends rode in the Salt to Saint Utah Bicycle Relay, so Kärstin and Kamie wrote this song for them and sang it at The Songwriting School of Los Angeles' open mic.

America the Beautiful

A good friend, Kärstin Lake, and I have been talking about collaborating for a while. She played this arrangement of America the Beautiful in church on Sept. 11 and I wanted to sing something patriotic this week, so this was the perfect opportunity!

Put Your Records On

Kamie covering her favorite Corinne Bailey Rae song. It reminds her to not take life too seriously—and has a great groove.

Gone Gone Gone

Seeing Phillip Phillips and his band play a few weeks ago nudged Kamie to transition from conceptualizing to doing. She decided to cover one of his well-loved songs this week and give him and his band a shout out and a thank you!

200 OK

A JavaScript inspired song that Kamie wrote making it's open mic debut at The Songwriting School of Los Angeles.

What makes employees smile at TowerJazz

Kamie worked on the concept for this video with Ela Pappo and Felize Richens then shot and edited it for the $1 billion run rate celebration.

A Laser Cut Record

In grad school, Kamie and Amy Lee started Cheeky Bean Studio to work with emerging mediums and concepts. Here is one of their creative experiments based on an Instructables tutorial.

Symmetrica

Symmetrica is based on Rorschach ink blot tests. Designer Richard Blake developed five laser cut headpieces to examine the fusion of fashion, graphic design, and psychology. Kamie photographed the pieces for Richard.

Learning about biological pest control with GAIA in Israel

Kamie and a classmate, Amy Lee Walton, received a research grant from Maryland Institute College of Art to attend, observe and learn about the collaborative structure and hands-on teaching methodologies as well as document the trip and create summary posters and videos.

Unravel the Code

Unravel the Code was the theme of an international collaboration wearable technology class and artist residency between the Maryland Institute College of Art (MICA) in Baltimore, and the Willem de Kooning Academy (WdKA) in Rotterdam, The Netherlands.

A Mural to Brighten the Child Development Center at St. Vincent Hospital

The forPEACE mural in Nazareth, Israel, was a four-and-a-half month collaborative project. Kamie traveled with the artists and documented and summarized the exchange.

A Silk Catalog for Tabitha Cambodia

When Kamie learned about the Tabitha Foundation in Cambodia and wanted to purchase products, she had a hard time knowing what the products looked like so she arranged photoshoots and created this catalog.

Bump. Set. Spike.

Getting the ball over the net is a team effort. This poster was part of a campaign for BYU Men's Volleyball that Kamie worked on with Dave Broberg and David Eberhard. Photographs by the talented Mark Philbrick.

11 Months

Never Stop Failing, a hip-hip group from Provo, Utah, featuring Jeff Wood and Chad Mustard, invited Kamie to sing on a couple tracks for an album in 2009. One of the songs, 11 Months, was featured in the hit XBOX Live Arcade game Shadow Complex.

I am BYU

There is more to athletes than their skills on the field. That was the idea behind this campaign for BYU womens' soccer that Kamie worked on with Dave Broberg and Weston Wride. Photographs once again by Mark Philbrick.
