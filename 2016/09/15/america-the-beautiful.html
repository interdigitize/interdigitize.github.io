America the Beautiful

Sep 15, 2016

A good friend, Kärstin Lake, and I have been talking about collaborating for a while. She played this arrangement of America the Beautiful in church on Sept. 11 and I wanted to sing something patriotic this week, so this was the perfect opportunity!

I have a hard time relating to some of the lyrics from Katharine Bates’ poem written in 1911, so I took creative liberty and made it more relatable.



LYRICS:

I see spacious skies
Past the power lines
And stores stocked with food
Mountain majesties
Feel like home to me
I know there so much more here I don't see

But I know we have a lot
And we want a lot
And we work a lot
But where is our heart where is our soul
In the moments that write the story of our
Home of the brave and land of the free

Trying to be great
And live what we believe
Some serve
Some take a knee
Just please don't feed me
Rubbish sleaze
I can't respect a political disease

Oh, America, America
God shed his grace on thee
And crown thy good with brotherhood
For all humanity!
