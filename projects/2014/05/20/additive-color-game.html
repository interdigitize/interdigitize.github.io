Additive Color Game

May 20, 2014   |  Tags: design

Taylor Nelson, Joshua Chavez and I came up with this mix-and-match color game to passively teach additive color theory.

Players swipe a red, green or blue square into another square to combine them to make a secondary or tertiary color. (If you are on a computer, instead of swiping, click the first square, then the second.) You earn points if you get two matching secondary or tertiary colors next to each other!

The goal is to see how many points you can earn before a timer reaches zero. As mixing colors becomes more familiar, focus on which blocks to swipe or click decreases and your score climbs.



Taylor, Josh and I jointly worked on the concept. Taylor headed the dev. I mocked up the design.
