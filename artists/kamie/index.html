Kamie is a storytelling creative working towards making unique audio visual experiences.

Kamie’s first love was music. Quitting piano lessons in suburban Utah propelled her into it. Practicing was no longer about playing to someone else’s perfection. Expressing and creating was the captivating gem.

Desiring to extend her storytelling passion, she studied Geography and Communications at Brigham Young University and Photographic and Electronic Media at the Maryland Institute College of Art (MICA).

Kamie currently lives in the LA area and continues to pursue merging audio, visual and digital components to explore concepts and tell stories. She is studying at The Songwriting School of Los Angeles in parallel to learning JavaScript.

Form in Motion
How you see someone in a moment is very different than how you see them after you have known them for a while.

Kamie's thesis work, Form in Motion, is an abstract photographic series depicting this concept. The long exposure photos of a person wearing a red, green and blue dress reveal a full spectrum of colors that can only be seen with movement and time.
īs
This photograph is from a series on fear about not understanding what you see and feeling threatened by it.

The title plays on this concept looking like "is" unless you know that the macron above the "i" indicates a long vowel making it "eyes". The subject matter falls inline looking like an eye but it is just a different perspective of plastic, metal and LEDs through a lens.
