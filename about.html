Kamie

   

My first love was music. Quitting piano lessons in suburban Utah propelled me into it. Practicing was no longer about playing to someone else’s perfection. Expressing and creating was the captivating gem.

Wanting to better understand different people and regions, I studied geography and communications at Brigham Young University. After graduation I took an internship with the nonprofit, forPEACE. Projects took me to Cambodia, Germany and Israel. To sharpen my creative skills so I could better share the stories I was living and learning, I went back to school and studied photographic and electronic media at the Maryland Institute College of Art (MICA). The liberal program pushed me to redefine my understanding of art and see beauty in abstraction. It also introduced me to microcontrollers and programming.

I now live in the LA area and am working on projects that merge audio, visual and digital components.

A band and two artists I would like to work with:

OK GO

OK GO's videos and music are a beautiful blend of captivating creativity and well-executed calculations. The One Moment, and This Too Shall Pass are especially impressive, but Needing/Getting is my favorite. Working on a video with them is a dream.

Aaron Koblin

Aaron is an artist, designer, and programmer specializing in data and digital technologies. He is also one of the founders of Within. I like the concepts he is working on around collaborative creation, like the Bicycle Built for 2,000 and The Wilderness Downtown.

Di Mainstone

Di is an artist and "Movician" that creates sonic sculptures (basically instruments) and uses them to compose sound through movement. Her artist talk at MICA got me thinking about an idea that I am still working toward.
