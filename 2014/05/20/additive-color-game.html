Additive Color Game

May 20, 2014

Taylor Nelson, Joshua Chavez and Kamie came up with this mix-and-match color game to passively teach additive color theory.

Players swipe a red, green or blue square into another square to combine them to make a secondary color. (If you are on a computer, click the first square, then the second.) Two matching secondary colors next to each other earns you points!

The goal is to see how many points you can get before time runs out.


We worked on this MVP at Angel Hack LA in May 2014.
